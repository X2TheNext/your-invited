Reading the Setup Diagrams

Every pose across all three guides includes a top-down blocking diagram. Here's how to read it.

Top-Down Blocking Key

Each diagram is a bird's-eye view of the scene: the dashed gold line along the top marks the backdrop (mural, storefront, stoop, skyline, etc.), with its name labeled above it. Subjects are placed and rotated to show which way they're facing — a short line with a dot marks the direction of their gaze or lean.

The camera icon shows where the photographer stands and which way the lens points (the faint triangle is the field of view). Sun-style icons mark light sources, with a dashed arrow showing the direction the light travels toward the subject(s).
