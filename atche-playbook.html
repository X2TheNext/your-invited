01 — Overview

The Opportunity

Creative women in nightlife — photographers, hosts, DJs, stylists, content creators — generate enormous cultural and economic value for venues, brands, and events. But they have no infrastructure that works for them. No platform to build credibility, no community that vets and supports them, no brand that treats their presence as the asset it actually is. ATCHÉ is that infrastructure.

"The talent is already here. The network is already here. The demand is already here. We're just building the room where it all connects." — Xavier Snow, Founder
🎯
The Gap
DC nightlife has no brand built around and for creative Black women. Events exist. Venues exist. Talent exists. The platform that organizes and monetizes that talent — and gives it a name — does not.
🏛
The Market
DC + HBCU corridor: Howard, Bowie, Morgan State, Hampton. 200,000+ students within driving distance. The most politically and culturally active Black college market in the country.
⚡
The Timing
Post-COVID nightlife is restructuring. Venues are actively seeking brands that bring consistent, curated crowds. ATCHÉ enters as a partner, not a renter — with leverage from day one.
🌐
The Network Effect
Every woman in the Circle recruits the next. Every event pulls the guest list tighter. Every brand activation validates the audience. This compounds — and it does so fast when the first room is right.
02 — Structure

Business Model

Three layers. Each one serves a different user and generates different revenue. The Circle is the supply side — creative talent. The Network is the demand side — brands and venues. The Room is where the transaction happens — events.

✦
The Circle
Vetted creative women. Join by application + referral. Photographers, hosts, DJs, stylists, content creators, brand ambassadors. They get: community, opportunities, bookings, credibility, ATCHÉ cosign.
Supply
◈
The Network
Venue partners and brand sponsors. They pay for access to a curated, high-quality audience and the talent that comes with it. Events, activations, sponsorships, and booking fees.
Demand
🏛
The Room
The events themselves. Tables, tickets, VIP packages, bottle service, door. Where the Circle performs, the Network activates, and the guests pay for the experience. This is where the flywheel spins.
Transaction
The Flywheel
Better talent → better events → better crowds → better brand deals → more money for talent → better talent. The Circle keeps quality high. Quality keeps venues committed. Venue commitment keeps talent paid. That loop is the moat. Once it starts turning, it's extremely difficult for a competitor to replicate.
03 — Money

Revenue Architecture

Six distinct revenue streams. Events are the engine. Memberships and brand deals are the fuel. Booking fees are the long game. Not all six will be active at launch — prioritize in this order.

Stream What It Is Pricing Launch Priority
Event Tables Reserved table packages at ATCHÉ events. Includes bottle service, priority entry, dedicated host. $300–600/table (4–6 guests) Week 1
Event Tickets General admission + VIP tickets. Pre-sale discounted, door full price. $30–40 pre-sale · $50–60 door Week 1
Brand Activations Sponsored moments at events — product placement, dedicated space, photo ops, branded experiences. $500–3,000/event Event 1
Referral Codes Partner brands give discount codes to attendees. ATCHÉ earns 10–15% commission on redemptions. 10–15% commission Event 1
Circle Membership Monthly or annual membership for active Circle women. Unlocks booking priority, event perks, community access. $35/mo · $350/yr Month 2
Talent Booking Fees When ATCHÉ places a Circle member in a booking (DJ set, host gig, photo shoot), ATCHÉ takes 10–15%. 10–15% of booking rate Month 3+
Venue Revenue Split — The Deal Structure
ATCHÉ owns the ticket and table revenue. The venue earns on bar sales. This is the standard nightlife partnership model, and it gives ATCHÉ full control over pricing and the guest experience.

Negotiate this hard: venue covers security, staffing, and sound. ATCHÉ covers production, marketing, and talent. Bar revenue stays with the venue. Ticket + table revenue stays with ATCHÉ.

05 — Territory

The Corridor

H Street to U Street. Two of DC's most culturally active corridors, separated by about 2 miles. ATCHÉ has relationships at anchor venues on both. This is not an accident — it's a territory strategy.

Anchor — H Street NE
Focus Social Club
H Street Corridor, NE Washington DC
The launch venue. Ranked #1 in the Venue CRM (94/100 ATCHÉ Score). Strong cultural fit, established crowd, open to partnership. ATCHÉ Fridays begins here. This is where the brand identity gets built in public for the first time.
Bridge
The 2-Mile Link
H St → Shaw → U St
The two corridors are deeply connected culturally. The same demographic moves between them. Building presence on both ends means ATCHÉ becomes synonymous with the entire DC creative nightlife scene, not just one block.
Expansion — U Street NW
Everyday People
U Street Corridor, NW Washington DC
The second venue. Already in the Venue CRM. Anchor for Phase 2 events and the natural home for the Cookies DC activation. Once Focus is locked, Everyday People becomes the next conversation — and the Cookies relationship makes that introduction easier.
Why This Matters Strategically
Most DC nightlife brands are venue-dependent — they exist only as long as the venue lets them. ATCHÉ's multi-venue strategy means the brand travels. Guests follow ATCHÉ, not the address. That's leverage. That's what makes you negotiable.
06 — Partnership

Cookies DC Partnership

Cookies DC operates at Everyday People on U Street. Xavier has a direct relationship with the brand through his video work. This is a warm deal — not a cold pitch. Structure it right and it becomes a recurring brand activation that validates ATCHÉ for every subsequent brand conversation.

What ATCHÉ Gives
Audience access — curated creative women, 21+, culturally aligned with Cookies' brand identity
Event placement — dedicated Cookies activation space at each event (lounge area, product display, brand moment)
Social amplification — ATCHÉ posts + stories featuring the partnership pre/during/post event
Referral code distribution — attendees get 15–20% off their next Cookies purchase at checkout
Content creation — Circle photographers capture the activation for Cookies' own use
What ATCHÉ Gets
Brand fee — $500–1,500 per event activation
Pre-event promotion — Cookies DC promotes ATCHÉ to their audience (U Street foot traffic + social)
Commission — 10–15% of every code redemption driven by ATCHÉ attendees
Credibility — a recognized brand associated with ATCHÉ from Event 1 makes the next brand pitch significantly easier
The U Street introduction — Cookies' relationship with Everyday People is the warm door to that venue partnership
How to Pitch It
Lead with the audience, not the event. "We're bringing 100–150 creative women to H Street and U Street every month. They're your customer. We want to make sure they know Cookies DC." Keep the ask simple for Event 1 — $500 flat + code distribution. Prove the concept. Renegotiate upward for Event 2.

13 — Risk

Known Bottlenecks

Knowing the failure modes in advance is the advantage. These are the four most likely points of stall — and the fix for each.

⚠️
Xavier as Single Point of Failure
Every deal, every creative call, every judgment flows through you right now. This breaks somewhere between Event 3 and Event 6.
Fix: Identify one Diamond-tier woman by Month 2 who can carry event ops. Start handing off tactical decisions by Month 3.
⚠️
First Event Quality
The first event sets the brand forever in DC. An underfilled room or the wrong crowd is harder to recover from than most people admit.
Fix: Soft cap at 100. Fill it completely before opening general sale. A packed 100-person room is 10x more powerful than a half-empty 200-person room.
⚠️
Venue Dependency
If Focus pulls the deal, ATCHÉ loses its launch platform. Single-venue dependency is existential risk at this stage.
Fix: Have Everyday People in active negotiation before Event 1. Never be one "no" away from no events.
⚠️
Community Culture Drift
The wrong 5 women admitted early can shift the room's culture in the wrong direction. Once it drifts, it's nearly impossible to pull back.
Fix: First 10 members are personal invites only. Application flow opens after the culture is set. Trust your read. The tracker is a tool — the judgment is yours.
