01

The Walk-Up Trio

U Street energyOpener shot
Reference

U Street corridor vibe — neon bar signage and rowhouse silhouettes blurred behind them.

Setup

Shoot from roughly waist height, dead-on as all three walk toward you arm-in-arm or shoulder-to-shoulder. Wide aperture / portrait mode to blur the background. Catch them mid-stride, matching energy and pace — middle person sets the tempo.

Why it works

Instant motion and group energy — strong first frame for any squad set.

Setup Diagram

All three walk toward camera in a loose row; shooter stays low at waist height, dead-on.

02

Triangle Lean

Shaw / Bloomingdale muralsRelaxed editorial
Reference

Any painted mural wall — Shaw, Bloomingdale, and Eaton-area alleys all work.

Setup

One person stands center against the wall, the other two lean in at angles on either side, creating a loose triangle. Shoot at eye level or slightly below. Heads turned toward camera, easy smiles, weight relaxed into the wall.

Why it works

Triangular composition naturally balances three bodies in frame — reads as effortless, not staged.

Setup Diagram

Center person against the wall, the other two angled in to form a triangle; shot at eye level.

03

Stoop Squad Trio

Rowhouse stoopCandid / warm
Reference

Classic DC rowhouse front steps — porch light on, staggered seating across the steps.

Setup

Seat the three across two or three step levels so every face is visible — no one directly blocking another. Drinks/cups optional. Shoot from slightly below looking up, or from across the street with more zoom. Get them talking to each other, not posing.

Why it works

Staggered height keeps the whole group visible and candid — feels like a real night out with the squad.

Setup Diagram

Staggered across the steps at different heights; camera low and off to the side, angled up.

04

Mirror Check Squad

Storefront windowQuick / repeatable
Reference

Any DC storefront with a large glass window — Georgetown, U Street, or 14th Street corridor all work.

Setup

All three standing in front of the window checking reflections together — one adjusting hair, the others glancing over or at each other. Shoot from outside at a slight angle so the whole group and their reflections land in frame. Under 10 seconds.

Why it works

Group version of the "mirror" trick — feels like a real pre-night-out moment with the whole crew.

Setup Diagram

Three across in front of the glass, reflections in frame; camera holds outside at a slight angle.

05

Look-Back Trio

Marquee entranceStory shot
Reference

Glowing marquee or club entrance — Howard Theatre-style signage and warm lights.

Setup

Have all three walk toward the entrance together; call out right as they reach the door for a synced look-back over the shoulder. Shoot from behind/side with the marquee glow as backlight. Stagger them slightly so all three faces are visible.

Why it works

Implies a story — the whole crew heading in together, and so is the viewer.

Setup Diagram

All three walk toward the marquee, then sync a look-back; camera shoots from behind/side, marquee glow as backlight.

06

Cheers Circle

Neon bokeh backdropBeauty detail
Reference

Any dark backdrop with ambient neon bokeh in the distance.

Setup

Heads tilted in toward a small circle, glasses clinked together at chin height in the center. Tight frame on all three faces. Rim light from one side — streetlight, neon sign, or phone flashlight in a pinch.

Why it works

Intimate beauty/detail shot that shows the friendship and the glam at once, scaled to a trio.

Setup Diagram

Loose circle, glasses clinked in the middle; rim light from one side, tight frame on all three.

07

Sidewalk Sunnies Squad

Daytime streetQuick / repeatable
Reference

Any DC sidewalk in daylight — rowhouses or storefronts in the background.

Setup

Standing side by side, all three lifting sunglasses slightly off the face in mirrored timing, direct eye contact, easy smiles. Shoot straight-on at eye level. Under 10 seconds — built for fast street approaches.

Why it works

The fastest squad pose in the set — matching energy reads instantly, ideal for daytime walkups.

Setup Diagram

Three across, mirrored sunglasses lift on cue; camera straight-on at eye level, flat daylight.

08

Triple Twirl

Mural backdropHigh energy
Reference

Bold-color mural wall — gives the fabric something vibrant to pop against.

Setup

Crouch for a low angle. Have all three spin together and call "now" right as the fabric flares. Space them out slightly so movement doesn't overlap. Burst mode if your camera has it — pick the best fabric shapes after.

Why it works

Movement plus color, tripled — usually the standout shot of the whole squad set.

Setup Diagram

All three spin together on cue; camera crouches low and close to catch the fabric flare.

09

Candid Laugh Circle

Bar / loungeAuthentic
Reference

Any warm-lit bar or lounge interior, drinks in hand.

Setup

Don't ask for a smile — get the group talking or reacting to each other for a real laugh. Medium close-up, slightly above eye level, arrange so all three faces are visible (avoid a straight line — angle them into a loose circle).

Why it works

Authenticity sells — every squad set needs one "real moment" between friends.

Setup Diagram

Loose circle, reacting to each other for a real laugh; camera slightly above eye level, warm ambient light.

10

Power Stance Trio

Bold mural, full bodyMain character
Reference

A bold, graphic DC mural with room for three full-body frames.

Setup

Full body, all three with hands on hips, feet planted shoulder-width, mirrored stances. Shoot straight-on at chest height, step back enough to fit everyone with even spacing. Check that the mural colors don't clash with any outfit.

Why it works

Triple "main character" energy — confidence and full fit visibility for the whole squad.

Setup Diagram

Full body, evenly spaced with hands on hips; camera pulls back and squares at chest height.

11

Glam Huddle

Club interior lightingEditorial polish
Reference

Club interior with magenta/blue ambient lighting.

Setup

Tight beauty shot — all three heads tilted in toward center, jewelry and makeup detail visible. Get close enough that the colored lighting wraps around every face. Shallow depth of field.

Why it works

The "magazine crop" group shot — polish, detail, and closeness in one frame.

Setup Diagram

Tight huddle, heads together; magenta and blue ambient light wraps all three faces.

12

Skyline Silhouette Trio

Monument / skyline viewCloser shot
Reference

Rooftop, bridge, or waterfront with a DC skyline or monument view at night.

Setup

Standing close together, expose for the skyline so all three go dark/silhouette. Find a rim light source (streetlight, building glow) to outline hair and shoulders on everyone.

Why it works

Ties the whole squad set back to place — strong closer for any DC shoot.

Setup Diagram

Standing close together, exposed for the skyline so all three go to silhouette; rim light from behind.
